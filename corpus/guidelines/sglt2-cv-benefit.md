title: SGLT2 inhibitors — cardiovascular and renal benefit
source: ADA / ESC consensus 2023 (paraphrased)
url: https://diabetesjournals.org/care/issue/47/Supplement_1
---
SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin, canagliflozin) reduce major adverse cardiovascular events and slow CKD progression in patients with type 2 diabetes and established cardiovascular disease, heart failure, or albuminuric CKD. Benefit is largely independent of glycemic improvement. They are recommended as add-on therapy or alongside metformin for these high-risk groups regardless of baseline A1c. Caveats: euglycemic DKA risk, volume depletion, increased mycotic genital infections.
