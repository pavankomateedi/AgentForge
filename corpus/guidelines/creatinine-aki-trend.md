title: Acute kidney injury — creatinine-based KDIGO criteria
source: KDIGO 2012 AKI Guideline (paraphrased)
url: https://kdigo.org/guidelines/acute-kidney-injury/
---
Acute kidney injury is defined by KDIGO as any of: (a) increase in serum creatinine ≥0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours, (b) increase to ≥1.5× baseline within the prior 7 days, or (c) urine output <0.5 mL/kg/h for 6 hours. Staging: stage 1 = 1.5–1.9× baseline or +0.3 mg/dL; stage 2 = 2.0–2.9× baseline; stage 3 = ≥3× baseline, creatinine ≥4.0 mg/dL, or initiation of renal replacement. A trend across encounters (e.g., 1.3 → 1.5 → 1.8 mg/dL within weeks) warrants evaluation regardless of single-value cut-off.
