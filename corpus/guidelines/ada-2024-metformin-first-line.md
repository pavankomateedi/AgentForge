title: Metformin as first-line glucose-lowering therapy in T2DM
source: ADA Standards of Care 2024 (paraphrased)
url: https://diabetesjournals.org/care/issue/47/Supplement_1
---
Metformin remains the preferred initial pharmacologic agent for type 2 diabetes when not contraindicated and tolerated. In patients with established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, heart failure, or chronic kidney disease, an SGLT2 inhibitor or GLP-1 receptor agonist with proven cardiovascular benefit should be added independent of A1c, often alongside metformin. Combination therapy at diagnosis is appropriate when A1c is significantly above goal (e.g., >1.5% above target).
