title: Penicillin allergy and cephalosporin cross-reactivity
source: AAAAI / ACP 2019 (paraphrased)
url: https://www.aaaai.org
---
Reported penicillin allergy is common (around 10% of inpatients), but most are not true IgE-mediated reactions; the majority of patients with a recorded label can safely tolerate beta-lactams after structured re-evaluation. Cross-reactivity between penicillins and cephalosporins is now estimated at approximately 1–2% — much lower than the historically cited 10%. First- and second-generation cephalosporins with side-chain similarity carry slightly higher risk; third- and fourth-generation cephalosporins (e.g., ceftriaxone, cefepime) and carbapenems carry minimal cross-reactivity.
