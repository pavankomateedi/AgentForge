---
chunk_id: metformin-ckd-contraindication
title: Metformin contraindication in advanced CKD
source: FDA labeling + KDIGO 2022 (paraphrased)
url: https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/
---
Metformin is contraindicated when the estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) is below 30 mL/min/1.73m². Use should be reassessed if eGFR falls below 45 mL/min/1.73m² — a dose reduction is typically warranted, and initiation in this range is generally not recommended. Lactic acidosis risk drives the threshold: clearance of metformin is renal, and accumulation in advanced kidney disease can be fatal. Alternative agents (DPP-4 inhibitors, GLP-1 agonists with renal dose adjustment, insulin) should be considered.
