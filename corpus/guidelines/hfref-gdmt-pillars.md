title: Heart failure with reduced EF — four-pillar GDMT
source: ACC/AHA/HFSA 2022 Heart Failure Guideline (paraphrased)
url: https://www.jacc.org/doi/10.1016/j.jacc.2021.12.012
---
For patients with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF, LVEF ≤40%), foundational guideline-directed medical therapy now consists of four drug classes initiated and titrated together: an angiotensin receptor–neprilysin inhibitor (ARNI; sacubitril/valsartan) preferred over ACEi/ARB, a beta-blocker proven in HFrEF (carvedilol, bisoprolol, metoprolol succinate), a mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (spironolactone or eplerenone), and an SGLT2 inhibitor (dapagliflozin or empagliflozin). Sequence is less important than completing all four within weeks; symptom and biomarker (NT-proBNP) trends guide titration.
