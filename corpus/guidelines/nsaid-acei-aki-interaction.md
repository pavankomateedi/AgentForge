title: NSAID + ACEi/ARB + diuretic — "triple whammy" AKI risk
source: NICE CKD guidance (paraphrased)
url: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203
---
The combination of a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID), an ACE inhibitor or ARB, and a diuretic is a recognized cause of acute kidney injury — sometimes termed the "triple whammy." NSAIDs constrict the afferent arteriole; ACEi/ARBs dilate the efferent arteriole; the resulting drop in glomerular pressure with concurrent diuretic-induced volume depletion can precipitate AKI especially in elderly patients, those with pre-existing CKD, or volume-depleted states. Avoid NSAIDs in patients on these agents; use acetaminophen or topical alternatives where possible.
