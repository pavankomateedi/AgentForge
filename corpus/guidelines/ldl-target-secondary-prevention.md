title: LDL-C target in secondary prevention
source: ACC/AHA 2018 + AACE 2020 (paraphrased)
url: https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000000625
---
For patients with established ASCVD on maximally tolerated statin therapy, an LDL-C threshold of ≥70 mg/dL is reasonable to add ezetimibe; ≥70 mg/dL on statin + ezetimibe is reasonable to add a PCSK9 inhibitor for very-high-risk patients. The lowest evidence-supported target in secondary prevention is ≤55 mg/dL for very-high-risk patients (recurrent events, multivessel CAD, polyvascular disease, diabetes with end-organ damage). Statin + ezetimibe + PCSK9-i achieves this in most patients.
