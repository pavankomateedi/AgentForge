title: ACEi/ARB therapy and hyperkalemia monitoring
source: KDIGO BP guideline 2021 (paraphrased)
url: https://kdigo.org/guidelines/blood-pressure-in-ckd/
---
ACE inhibitors and angiotensin receptor blockers reduce aldosterone-mediated potassium excretion and can precipitate hyperkalemia, especially in patients with CKD (eGFR <60), diabetes, congestive heart failure, or concurrent potassium-sparing medications (spironolactone, eplerenone, trimethoprim). Baseline serum potassium and creatinine should be measured before starting therapy and within 1–2 weeks of dose changes. K+ ≥5.5 mEq/L typically requires dose reduction or discontinuation; values ≥6.0 mEq/L warrant urgent intervention.
