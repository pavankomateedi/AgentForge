title: BNP / NT-proBNP — diagnostic thresholds in suspected HF
source: AHA/HFSA / ESC guidance (paraphrased)
url: https://www.jacc.org/doi/10.1016/j.jacc.2021.12.012
---
In acute dyspnea evaluation, BNP <100 pg/mL or NT-proBNP <300 pg/mL effectively rule out heart failure. NT-proBNP thresholds for "rule-in" are age-adjusted: <50 yrs ≥450 pg/mL, 50–75 yrs ≥900 pg/mL, >75 yrs ≥1800 pg/mL. Renal dysfunction elevates NT-proBNP; obesity lowers it — interpret values in clinical context. Trends over weeks (e.g., halving) generally correlate with successful HF management; rising NT-proBNP despite GDMT prompts re-evaluation.
