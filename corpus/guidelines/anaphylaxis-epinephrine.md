title: Anaphylaxis — epinephrine first-line
source: AAAAI / WAO Anaphylaxis Guideline (paraphrased)
url: https://www.aaaai.org
---
Intramuscular epinephrine into the mid-anterolateral thigh (1 mg/mL solution; adult dose 0.3–0.5 mg, pediatric 0.01 mg/kg up to 0.3 mg) is the first-line therapy for anaphylaxis and should be administered without delay when the diagnosis is suspected. Antihistamines and corticosteroids are adjuncts only and never substitute for epinephrine. A second dose may be given after 5–15 minutes if response is inadequate. All patients require observation for biphasic reaction (typically 4–6 hours, longer if severe). Patients with documented severe drug allergy (e.g., anaphylactic reaction to penicillin) warrant epinephrine auto-injector prescription and an Allergy referral.
