title: Warfarin + NSAID — major bleeding risk
source: ACCP / CHEST guideline (paraphrased)
url: https://journal.chestnet.org
---
Concomitant warfarin and NSAID use roughly doubles to triples the risk of major gastrointestinal bleeding. The mechanism is dual: NSAIDs inhibit platelet aggregation and damage gastric mucosa while warfarin impairs the secondary hemostatic response. Where analgesia is unavoidable, acetaminophen at ≤2 g/day is preferred (higher doses may modestly elevate INR). If NSAID use is essential, gastroprotection with a proton pump inhibitor and tight INR monitoring are warranted; a direct oral anticoagulant (DOAC) is not safer in this context.
