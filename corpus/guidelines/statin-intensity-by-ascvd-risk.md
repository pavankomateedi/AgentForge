title: Statin intensity selection by ASCVD risk
source: ACC/AHA 2018 Cholesterol Guideline (paraphrased)
url: https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000000625
---
Patients with established clinical ASCVD should receive high-intensity statin therapy (atorvastatin 40–80 mg or rosuvastatin 20–40 mg) for an LDL-C reduction ≥50%. For primary prevention with 10-year ASCVD risk ≥20%, high-intensity statin is also recommended. Risk 7.5%–<20% qualifies for moderate-intensity statin after a clinician–patient risk discussion (consider risk enhancers: diabetes, CKD, family history, elevated Lp(a), inflammatory markers). Diabetes ages 40–75 with risk ≥20% qualifies for high-intensity statin.
