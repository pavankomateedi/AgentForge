title: Stage 2 hypertension — immediate two-drug therapy
source: ACC/AHA 2017 Hypertension Guideline (paraphrased)
url: https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/HYP.0000000000000065
---
For adults with stage 2 hypertension (≥140/90 mmHg) or with average BP more than 20/10 mmHg above goal, initial therapy with two first-line agents from different classes is recommended, often as a single combination tablet to support adherence. Preferred first-line classes: thiazide diuretics, ACEi or ARB, calcium-channel blockers. In Black adults without HF or CKD, a thiazide or CCB is preferred as first agent. ACEi and ARB should not be used together.
